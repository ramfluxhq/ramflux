"Ramflux", the Ramflux name, and the Ramflux logo are trademarks of Span Brain.
The open-source licenses that cover the code (see `LICENSING.md`) grant
you broad rights to use, modify, and redistribute the **software** — but they do
**not** grant any rights to the Ramflux **name or logo**.

## What you may do

- Use the name nominatively to refer to the Ramflux project — e.g. "compatible
  with Ramflux", "built on Ramflux", "a Ramflux client".
- State, truthfully, that your product uses or is derived from Ramflux.

## What you must not do

- Use the Ramflux name or logo as the name of your fork, product, or service in
  a way that suggests it is the official Ramflux project or is endorsed by it.
- Use a confusingly similar name or logo.

## Forks

If you distribute a modified version, please **rebrand it** — choose your own
name and logo so users are not confused about which project they are running and
trusting. This is especially important for a security and privacy product.

Questions about trademark use: security@spanbrain.org.
